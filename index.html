Tornen les Esquelles

Publication

2023

This publication narrates the experience lived by the participants in the project 'Tornen les esquelles' ('The cowbells are back'), which seeks to revitalise the complex situation of shepherds and their flocks in the Collserola Natural Park. It also highlights the importance of wool as a natural resource and seeks to preserve and revitalise the pastoral culture here. The cover paper is made of Collserola's Wool. And inside the book the text plays with the idea of "Rivers of white" in the typography in order to emulate the paths the Sheeps walktrough.

Persona, Foto, Copia

Exposición Julia Spínola. Artium

2023

Starting from a creative process that seeks to question relationships with other agents, design reflects on the designer's agency. The collaborative spaces that allow for the creation of a new imaginary, along with the process of creating graphic pieces, challenge the existence of a privileged center from which other images are distributed. From a queer, xeno perspective in the design process, the existence of the window is uncovered and revealed; the process becomes evident and, therefore, so does the window through which we perceive. In the process, as it becomes open, we allow ourselves to be shaken by external interactions; it appears, it becomes visible. The moment the process ceases to be normative, we become aware of its existence, and the window is revealed. The graphic, therefore, will never be considered finished; instead, it will show a specific combination and interaction that exists and makes sense based on the interaction of agents at this moment. It is a snapshot from the window, of this moment. In future possible iterations, the waves in the pool will be different, leading us to new forms, deformations, and interactions that make visible the existence of the multiplicity of agents that belong to the plane of immanence, as well as their windows of perception.

Mycelium driven Brand

Eina Bosc

2024

The graphic design is inspired by the symbols historically used to mark sheep, identifying their ownership and distinguishing them when they grazed among different flocks. It blends these archetypal, ancestral, and mysterious images with the contemporary use of spray paint, a tool now used to mark animals. These symbols also serve as the foundation and inspiration for creating a custom typography.
